  • 01Show the action. Every role's landing view is dominated by what requires their immediate attention — pending approvals, open corrections, awaiting decisions.

01
Standard User
Initiator
"What is happening with the forms I submitted?"
Access scope
Own requests only Department forms Submission status
Central question on login
What is happening with the forms I submitted?
Their world
A workspace centered on their own requests
The initiator's environment answers one question the moment they log in. Status is visible at a glance — no navigation required. They see where each request sits in the approval chain and which approver currently holds it.
What they focus on
Status and correction
  • ⏳ Requests pending approval
  • ↩ Requests returned for correction
  • ✓ Requests fully completed
  • ✗ Requests rejected
  • + Forms available to create
Key UX requirement
No exploration required
The initiator should never need to dig through menus to find their request status. The system surfaces active state immediately. Departmental form access is pre-filtered — they never see forms irrelevant to their department. Approval chain position is always visible per request.
5-minute success
What they know before logging out
  • Which requests are waiting for approval and with whom
  • Whether any request requires their correction
  • Whether any request was rejected and why
  • The full position of each request in the approval chain
  • Can start a new request if needed — with one action
02
Decision Maker
Approver
"What approvals are waiting for me?"
Core actions
Approve Reject Return for correction View chain
Central question on login
What approvals are waiting for me right now?
Their world
The pending approvals queue dominates
The approver's environment is built around the queue of decisions awaiting action. Count, form type, and chain progress are immediately visible. They understand what came before them and what follows after — giving full context before acting.
Three focus areas
Queue · History · Progress
  • !
    Pending Approvals — requests currently awaiting their decision
  • H
    Approval History — requests already approved or rejected
  • →
    Chain Progress — full visibility of approval sequence per request
Key UX requirement
Context before action
Before an approver acts, they must understand the full picture. The system shows who approved before them, who approves after them, and all relevant request details. The goal of every session is a cleared approval queue — the interface reinforces this through visual count indicators and completion states.
5-minute success
What they accomplish before logging out
  • All pending requests identified and reviewed
  • Approval chain context understood per request
  • Approve, reject, or return decision made
  • Approval queue cleared — zero pending items remain
03
System Owner
Administrator
"How is the system running? What needs my attention?"
Control scope
Users Departments Forms Approvals Workflows Reporting
Central question on login
Is the system healthy, and does anything require my intervention?
Their world
The control center of the organisation
The administrator has the broadest view of any user. Their workspace balances three concerns: monitoring what is happening now, configuring how the system behaves, and reporting on historical activity across all departments.
System health snapshot
Four operational metrics always visible
  • ▦Active forms in the system
  • ⏳Forms pending approval
  • ✓Forms fully completed
  • ✗Forms rejected
Configuration control
What they manage
User accounts and permissions, department hierarchy, form definitions, approval templates, and organisational workflows. Approval paths are viewable visually — the administrator understands the full routing logic without needing to inspect individual records.
Workspace balance
Three parallel concerns
  • 👁Operational monitoring — live system state
  • ⚙System configuration — forms, users, workflows
  • 📊Reporting & oversight — historical data
5-minute success
What they confirm before logging out
  • System operational health confirmed — no stuck workflows
  • Approval flows running as configured
  • User or permission changes made if required
  • Form or workflow configuration adjusted if needed
  • Always feels in control of the system — never surprised
04
CEO / CFO
Executive
"What decisions require my attention? Nothing else."
UX principle
Minimal interface Decision-only No dashboards
Central question on login
What decisions require my attention?
Their world
Focused. Simple. Fast.
Executives interact with FlowDesk only when their approval is required. The interface must not present operational metrics, configuration options, or system noise. Their session is a clean approval experience — in, decide, out.
Design requirement
No complexity. No friction.
The executive view suppresses all non-essential elements. There are no dashboards, no workflow configuration, no reporting panels. The system is explicitly designed so that an executive can complete their responsibilities in a single, brief interaction.
Critical UX note
This role's experience is about respect for time
Every additional element shown to an executive is a failure of design. The system should feel like a concierge that has pre-filtered everything, presented only what requires a decision, and made the path to action one tap away. Quick, efficient, and conclusive.
5-minute success
What they accomplish before logging out
  • All requests requiring their approval are immediately visible
  • Request context reviewed at a glance
  • Approve or reject decision completed
  • Session complete — they leave with decisions done
05
Visibility Only
Observer
"I need to view and access — not act."
Capabilities
View completed forms Download documents Access attachments
Central question on login
Where are the completed documents I need to access?
Their world
A read-only reference library
Observers are notified about certain requests but have no approval power. Their workspace is a curated view of completed forms relevant to them. The experience is focused on retrieval — not workflow interaction.
Key distinction
No workflow interaction
Observers do not approve, reject, or submit forms. They have no queue, no action buttons, no correction flows. Their access is intentionally scoped to visibility and document download only. The interface reflects this — there are no decision affordances shown to this role.
5-minute success
What they accomplish before logging out
  • Completed forms relevant to them are located quickly
  • Final documents downloaded or reviewed
  • Attachments accessed without navigating the full system

Design principles
01
Clarity of workflow
No user should ever be uncertain about what has happened, what is happening, or what happens next. Status is always visible.
02
Minimal complexity
Each role sees only what is relevant to their function. Irrelevant options, forms, and metrics are never surfaced.
03
Decision-first dashboards
Landing experiences are dominated by what requires action — not by metrics, history, or navigation structures.
04
Departmental access
Form access is pre-scoped by department. Users never encounter forms outside their operational context.
05
Sequential approvals
Approval chains are ordered, predictable, and visible to all stakeholders. No ambiguity about sequence or current position.
06
Full transparency
Progress is never hidden. Initiators, approvers, and administrators always understand the full lifecycle of every request.
